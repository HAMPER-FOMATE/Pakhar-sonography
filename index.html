Frequently Asked Questions

  • 1. Can ultrasound (sonography) harm me?

    No. Ultrasound is completely safe. It uses sound waves, not radiation, and has no known harmful effects when performed by trained professionals.

  • 2. Can pregnant women undergo ultrasound at Prakhar Sonography Centre?

    Yes. Ultrasound is the safest imaging method during pregnancy. It is routinely used to monitor the health and growth of the baby. Please bring your doctor’s prescription for the recommended scan.

  • 3. Do I need to wear any special protective equipment for ultrasound?

    No. Ultrasound is radiation-free, so no protective apron or special clothing is required. You may be asked to expose the area being scanned for accurate imaging.

  • 4. Which is better, an MRI or an ultrasound?

    Both tests serve different purposes. Ultrasound is ideal for imaging soft tissues, pregnancy, abdomen, pelvis, and blood flow. MRI is used when more detailed internal images are needed. Your doctor will decide which test is appropriate based on your condition.

  • 5. How do I book an appointment?

    You can book an appointment by calling our reception at [Your Phone Number] or by clicking the “Book Now” button on our website. Walk-ins are accepted, but appointments are given priority.

  • 6. Is ultrasound safe for babies and adults?

    Yes. Ultrasound is safe for all age groups—including unborn babies—because it uses harmless sound waves instead of radiation.

  • 7. How should I prepare for my ultrasound scan?

    Preparation depends on the type of ultrasound.

    Abdominal ultrasound: Fasting for 6–8 hours may be required.
    Pelvic or pregnancy scan: A full bladder may be needed.

    Our staff will guide you with specific instructions when you schedule your scan.

  • 8. When will I receive my ultrasound results?

    A radiologist reviews your ultrasound images and prepares a report, usually within 24 hours. Your referring doctor will explain the results to you.

  • 9. Do I need a doctor’s prescription for an ultrasound?

    Yes. A doctor’s referral is required for all diagnostic ultrasound tests to ensure the correct scan is performed.

  • 10. How long does an ultrasound exam take?

    Most ultrasound exams take 15–30 minutes, depending on the area being examined. Please arrive a little early for registration and preparation.

  • 11. Can I bring someone with me during the ultrasound?

    Yes, you may bring one adult companion. However, for safety and to maintain a calm environment, small children should wait in the reception area with another adult.
